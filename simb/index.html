Player's Guide

Welcome to SimBusiness

SimBusiness is a turn-based business simulation. Each turn represents one business day. Your decisions shape the outcome — the AI game master generates events and narrative based on what actually happened.

The Core Loop

  • Set your price — higher price means more revenue per sale, but affects demand
  • Buy inventory — you can't sell what you don't have; item1 is your primary product
  • Assign tasks — giving your team a task boosts their morale this turn
  • Run the turn — the sim engine runs, Claude writes the story, results appear
  • Review and repeat — learn, adjust, keep the business alive

Quick Tips

  • Watch your cash — running out means hard days ahead
  • Keep morale high by assigning tasks and paying fair wages
  • The demand index and reputation compound over time — invest in them early
  • Hire carefully — payroll is your biggest daily expense

How Demand Works

Customer demand = Base (50) × Demand Index × Weather × Reputation modifier. You sell the minimum of demand and available inventory. Weather is random each day (0.5–1.5×). Reputation grows slowly with good events and shrinks with bad ones.

Financials Each Turn

  • Revenue = units sold × your selling price
  • Expenses = daily payroll + inventory purchases + fixed operating cost ($12.50/day)
  • Profit/Loss = Revenue − Expenses (goes directly to cash balance)

COGS & Margin

Each unit sold consumes: 1× item1, 0.8× item2, 0.5× item3. The Decisions screen shows your Cost of Goods per sale and gross margin so you can price intelligently.

AI Events

After the math runs, Claude generates 1–3 events (good or bad) that can shift cash (±$40), reputation (±0.08), or demand index (±0.08). Events are bounded so they can't swamp the math.

Employee Stats

  • Morale (1–100) — emotional state. High morale improves event outcomes. Low morale hurts.
  • Burnout (0–100) — fatigue. At 100, imposes −5 morale every turn until burnout drops.
  • Dedication (1–10) — work ethic multiplier, affects morale gain from tasks.
  • Loyalty (1–10) — likelihood to stay through bad patches.
  • Experience (1–10) — skill level; affects AI event quality for that employee.

Salary & Market Rates

Market rates: Manager $90/day, Worker $65/day, Back Office $70/day. Pay below 80% of market rate and your employee gets −5 morale per turn (😞 Underpaid). Pay above 120% and they get +3 morale per turn (⭐ Motivated). Cutting someone's salary triggers a one-time −10 morale penalty.

Assigning Tasks

Write a specific task for any employee on the Decisions screen. Having a task gives +2 morale modifier and slight burnout increase. No task gives −1 morale modifier and slight burnout decrease.

Simulation Credits

Each turn costs 10 simulation credits. You start with 100 credits (10 turns). Credits represent the AI inference cost of running the game master each turn.

When your credits reach 0, you can no longer run turns. The game doesn't end — you just can't advance until you top up.

Credit Display

Your current credit balance is always shown in the top bar. The turn results screen shows exactly how many credits that turn consumed.
